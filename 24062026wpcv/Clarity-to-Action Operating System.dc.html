Before you buy

Common
questions.

How is this different from a regular self-help workbook?

Most workbooks end at reflection. This one is built specifically to convert reflection into a checkable, time-bound plan — the same way professional project design moves from a Theory of Change to a monitored logframe. Stage 4 is the entire reason this product exists.

How long does it take to complete?

Stages 1–3 typically take 60–90 minutes in one sitting. Stage 4 is best done after letting your diagnosis settle for a day or two. The full 90-day cycle is meant to be lived, not rushed.

What if I don't have a clear "problem" — I just feel vaguely stuck?

That's exactly what Stage 1 is built for. Most people start with a diffuse feeling and leave with a specific, scored baseline. A genuine audit almost always surfaces at least one domain that's clearly lower than the rest.

Do I need to complete it in order?

Yes — each stage builds on the last. Stage 4 specifically requires what you carried forward from Stages 1–3. The system is sequential by design.
